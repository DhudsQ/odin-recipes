French Toast

Story of the french toast

French toast, also known as pain perdu, has its origins in Ancient Rome. The Romans used stale bread, soaking it in a mixture of milk and eggs before frying it. During the Middle Ages, European cooks repurposed day-old bread, moistening it with eggs and frying it to create this delightful dish. Today, French toast remains a beloved comfort food enjoyed worldwide.

Ingredients

  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (Optional)
  • Â¼ teaspoon ground cinnamon (Optional)
  • salt to taste
  • 6 thick slices bread
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, or more as needed

Preparation steps

  1. Gather all ingredients
  2. Whisk milk, eggs, vanilla, cinnamon, and salt together in a shallow bowl
  3. Lightly butter a griddle or skillet and heat over medium-high heat
  4. Dunk bread in the egg mixture, soaking both sides
  5. Transfer to the hot skillet and cook until golden, 3 to 4 minutes per side
  6. Serve hot
