Lasagna

Story of the Lasagna

Lasagna, a beloved Italian dish, has ancient origins. While its roots are debated, the lasagna we know today emerged in Naples during the Middle Ages. The term “lasagna” comes from the Greek word “Laganon,” referring to layered pasta and sauce. Over centuries, the recipe evolved, incorporating cheese, eggs, and tomatoes. The classic version, “lasagna alla bolognese,” features thin pasta layers, ragù sauce, béchamel, and Parmigiano-Reggiano.

Preparation steps

  1. Make the meat sauce
  2. Cook the noodles
  3. Make the ricotta mixture
  4. Layer the lasagna in this order
    1. Meat Sauce
    2. Noodles
    3. Ricotta mixture
    4. Mozzarella slices
    5. Parmesean cheese
    6. Repeat the layers, then top with the reamining parmesean
  5. Cover with foil and bake
  6. Let the lasagna rest before serving
